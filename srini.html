Dr. Rajagopalan Srinivasan

Principal Innovator, CALIPER & CANARY Technologies
Mayo Clinic College of Medicine
Rochester, Minnesota, USA.

A research evangelist and self-professed life scientist with three decades of multi-disciplinary research, Dr. Rajagopalan collaborates with a multi-disciplinary team of scientists, engineers, clinicians and students to develop unique clinical frameworks for automatic understanding of radiologic images and triaging the patients to the most relevant clinical pathway. This paradigm has shown promise in a number of specialties including pulmonology, gastroenterology, orthopedic surgery, neurology, hepatology, and nephrology. Dr. Rajagopalan has also developed around twenty radiomics applications that are routinely used in clinical care worldwide.


Dr. Rajagopalan’s PhD dissertation work in tissue engineering at Mayo Clinic College of Medicine is a landmark achievement in marrying mathematics, biomaterials and biology. He has also made pioneering contributions in image guided neurosurgery through the development of high resolution digital brain atlas that is currently integrated into major image guidance systems. Dr. Rajagopalan has published more than 250 peer reviewed journal and conference publications, filed more than 25 international patents of which 15 have already been issued. Dr. Rajagopalan has won numerous awards from international societies including IEEE, MICCAI, Society for Biomaterials, Radiology Society of North America (RSNA), European Congress of Radiology (ECR), American Thoracic Society (ATS) and SPIE. He has also won corporate awards from IBM, General Electric and Mayo Clinic.


Prior to joining Mayo Clinic, Dr. Rajagopalan has worked for IBM Singapore, Center for Information Enhanced Medicine- a JHU, National University of Singapore joint venture, and General Electric Global research. He has collaborated with scientists from Harvard, Stanford, MIT, Purdue university, Fraunhaufer Institute and Massachusetts General Hospital. Dr. Srinivasan Rajagopalan’s technical contributions spans across parallel computing, high speed imaging, image based metrology, multi-dimensional image analysis, fuzzy logic and pattern recognition. He has successfully deployed his technical skills to further the practice of conventional and interventional radiology, cardiology, neurology, adult and pediatric pulmonology, ophthalmology, and tissue engineering.
